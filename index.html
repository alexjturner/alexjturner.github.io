Welcome

I will be starting as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Atmospheric Sciences at the University of Washington in January 2021.

I am a Miller Postdoctoral Fellow working with Ron Cohen and Inez Fung at the University of California at Berkeley. I work on a variety of topics related to atmospheric chemistry, the carbon cycle, remote sensing, and inverse modeling. Broadly, my research focuses on understanding how carbon cycles through different reservoirs in the earth system. An underlying theme of my work is quantifying the impacts of variations in atmosheric chemistry on the carbon cycle and, conversely, bringing insights from the carbon cycle into atmospheric chemistry.
